# Building Contributors Garden: Community-Driven Development

Contributors Garden started as a “what if” during The Developer Show: what if we let the audience literally plant ideas while we built live? The answer was a multiplayer pixel plot that changed every few minutes, a ton of chaos, and a community that felt co-creative instead of passive.

## The Workshop

I ran the project like a workshop inside a stream—pairing prompts (“What if we invited more bees?”) with new branches of the app. Viewers submitted pull requests, modded sprites, and guided priorities through emoji votes.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Short feedback loops:** A visible backlog on stream helped people understand what to work on.
2. **Narration beats polish:** Explaining tradeoffs while building kept everyone engaged, even when bugs popped up.
3. **Celebrate merges:** Shout-outs plus a simple badge kept energy high.

## Why it Matters

Community-driven demos change how people feel about developer tooling. When folks see their ideas ship in minutes, they trust the platform—and stick around for the next build.
