# Mapping AI Governance: Three Layers

I am mapping how access to compute, the infrastructure supply chain, and the people who set policy intersect. This is a quick share-out of the working model I'm using inside the fellowship.

## Layer 1 — Compute Access

Who gets priority access to high-capacity GPUs? Right now it's mostly hyperscalers and a handful of labs. That shapes who can experiment, who can ship, and who writes the narratives about capability. I'm documenting pathways for civic technologists to plug into these compute pools through public institutions and coalitions.

## Layer 2 — Infrastructure Choices

Infrastructure is downstream of politics, but it also sets the menu of what is possible. Everything from data center siting to export controls creates constraints. I'm interviewing the practitioners who translate policy into Terraform and Kubernetes manifests to understand where developers already exercise soft power.

## Layer 3 — Policy Windows

There are real windows right now—in the UK AI Safety Summit follow-ups, in EU AI Act implementation, and in U.S. procurement conversations. Developers are rarely in the room, so I'm building briefs that help them bring grounded stories about deployment tradeoffs.

## What's Next

I'll publish deeper dives on each layer, but for now I'm continuing to gather interviews and annotate the landscape. If you work on civic tech or have thoughts about compute governance, I'd love to hear from you.
